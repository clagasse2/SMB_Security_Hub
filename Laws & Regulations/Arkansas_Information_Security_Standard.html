Arkansas Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization to implement and maintain reasonable security procedures and practices appropriate to the nature of the information to protect personal information from unauthorized access, destruction, use, modification, or disclosure

Subject Entities Individuals, businesses, and state agencies. It does not apply to persons or businesses regulated by and compliant with state or federal laws with more stringent requirements for the protection of personal informatio
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • Violations of statute enforceable by the Attorney General pursuant to Ark. Code 4-88-101, et seq
  • Violations of the statute may constitute deceptive trade practices. Attorney General may seek an injunctive relief or restitution. Willful or knowing violations of statute may be subject to criminal penalties (Class A misdemeanor)
How to Comply
  • Records containing personal information may be destroyed by shredding, erasing, or otherwise modifying to make the personal information unreadable or undecipherable
  • This staute doesn't define reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
Exceptions Does not apply to persons or businesses regulated by and compliant with state or federal laws with more stringent requirements for the protection of personal information
