New Mexico Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization to implement and maintain reasonable security procedures and practices to protect personal identifying information from unauthorized access, destruction, use, modification or disclosure

Subject Entities Persons or entities that own or license personal identifying information of a New Mexico resident. Not applicable to state or local entities
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • A violation may be subject to injunction or damages for actual costs or losses, including consequential losses. Knowing or reckless violations may be subject to additional penalties up to $25,000
  • The Attorney General may bring an action for violation of the statute
How to Comply
  • Must require third-party service providers by contract to implement and maintain reasonable security procedures and practices appropriate to the nature of the personal information disclosed. Must be reasonably designed to help protect the information from unauthorized access, use, modification, disclosure, or destruction
  • This staute doesn't define reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
Exceptions Provisions do not apply to entities subject to Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act or HIPAA
