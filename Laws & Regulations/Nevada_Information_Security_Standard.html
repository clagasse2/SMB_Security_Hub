Nevada Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires organization to maintain reasonable security measures

Subject Entities A “data collector” means any government entity, institution of higher education, corporation, financial institution, retail operator, or other business entity or association that handles, collects, disseminates, or otherwise deals with nonpublic personal information
Penalty for Non-Compliance The Attorney General or any district attorney can bring an action to obtain a temporary or permanent injunction against any person believed to be in violation of the statute
How to Comply
  • A business must take reasonable measures to ensure the destruction of records containing customers’ personal information when it decides to no longer maintain them. Reasonable measures render the personal information in the records unreadable or undecipherable (such as shredding or erasing the information)
  • If a data collector discloses personal information to a third party pursuant to a contract, the contract must require the third party to comply with the statute’s security requirements
  • A data collector that does not accept payment card transactions must not electronically transfer any personal information other than via a facsimile outside its secure system unless it is encrypted. Similarly, it may not move a storage device containing personal information beyond its logical or physical control or the control of its data storage contractor or other party unless it is encrypted
  • This staute doesn't define reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
More Information Nevada Rev. Stat. §§ 603A.200–217
