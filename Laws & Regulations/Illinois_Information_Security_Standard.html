Illinois Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization to implement and maintain reasonable security measures to protect records that contain personal information concerning an Illinois resident from unauthorized access, acquisition, destruction, use, modification, or disclosure

Subject Entities Applies to all businesses, governmental agencies, and any other entities that otherwise handles, collects, disseminates, or otherwise deals with nonpublic personal information under the statute
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • A violation constitutes an unlawful practice under the Consumer Fraud and Deceptive Business Practices Act (815 ILCS §505/1 et seq.)
  • A violation may result in civil penalties and other remedies
  • $100 per person whose personal information was improperly disposed of, up to $50,000 for each instance of improper disposal
  • Attorney General may impose penalties
How to Comply
  • An organization must comply with the Illinois Data Disposal Standard
    • Paper documents containing personal information may be either redacted, burned, pulverized, or shredded so that personal information cannot practicably be read or reconstructed
    • Electronic media and other non-paper media containing personal information may be destroyed or erased so that personal information cannot practicably be read or reconstructed
  • The organization complies with a state or federal law providing a greater protection to personal information concerning an Illinois resident
  • The organization complies with the standards established pursuant to Section 501(b) of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act of 1999 (15 U.S.C. §6801)
  • Otherwise it is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
