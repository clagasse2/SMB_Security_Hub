Florida Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization must take reasonable measures to protect and secure electronic data containing personal information

Subject Entities Applies to business, corporations, government entities, other organizations, and any other third-party agent that acquires, maintains, stores, or uses personal information as defined by the statute
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • A covered entity that fails to provide the required consumer or regulatory notice under the statue may be liable for a civil penalty as follows: $1,000 per day for the first 30 days, $50,000 for each subsequent 30-day period up to 180 days, and a total amount not to exceed $500,000 if the violation continues for more than 180 days
  • A violation is treated as an unfair or deceptive trade practice in an action brought by the Florida Department of Legal Affairs. The statute does not establish a private right of action
How to Comply
  • Personal information may be disposed of by involving shredding, erasing, or otherwise modifying the personal information in the records to make it unreadable or undecipherable
  • This staute doesn't define "reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
