Kansas Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization to implement and maintain reasonable procedures and practices appropriate to the nature of the information, and exercise reasonable care to protect the personal information from unauthorized access, use, modification, or disclosure

Subject Entities Applies to all individuals, business, governmental agencies, and other entities that, in the ordinary course of business, collect, maintain or possess, or cause to be collected, maintained or possessed, the personal information of any other person
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • The Attorney General has sole enforcement authority. No private right of action
  • Each violation of this section shall be an unconscionable act or practice in violation of the Kansas Consumer Protection Act (Kan. Stat. Ann. § 50-627). Each record that is not destroyed in compliance with the statute shall constitute a separate unconscionable act
  • Not more than $10,000 for each violation under the Consumer Protection Act
How to Comply
  • This staute doesn't define "reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
  • Must take reasonable steps to destroy or arrange for the destruction of any records within custody or control containing personal information when such holder no longer intends to maintain or possess such records. Destruction must be by shredding, erasing, or otherwise modifying the personal identifying information in the records to make it unreadable or undecipherable through any means
