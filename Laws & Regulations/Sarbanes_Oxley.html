Sarbanes Oxley

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation ensures that public companies produce accurate financial statements

Subject Entities SOX applies to all US public companies and the Certified Public Accountants (CPAs) and CPA firms that provide them with auditing services
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • SOX penalties are mostly aimed at company officers like the CEO and CFO. If a CEO or CFO knowingly certifies a periodic report that does not satisfy the requirements of the Act, he or she is subject to fines of up to $1 million and imprisonment for up to 10 years. If he or she falsifies the certification willfully, the fine may be up to $5,000,000 and imprisonment up to 20 years
  • An organization can also lose it's exchange listing
How to Comply
  • Implement internal controls to protect financial data from tampering
  • File regular reports with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) attesting to the effectiveness of security controls and the accuracy of financial disclosures
