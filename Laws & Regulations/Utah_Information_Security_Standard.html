Utah Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires that an organization Must implement and maintain reasonable procedures to (1) prevent unlawful use or disclosure of personal information collected or maintained in the regular course of business; and (2) destroy, or arrange for the destruction of, records containing personal information that will no longer be retained

Subject Entities Any person who conducts business in Utah and maintains personal information
Penalty for Non-Compliance Penalties up to $2,500 for a violation or series of violations concerning a specific consumer and no greater than $100,000 in total, unless the violation concerns 10,000 or more consumers or the subject entity agrees to settle for a greater amount. A civil action for violations must occur within five years of the alleged violation; an administrative action must commence within 10 years of the alleged violation
How to Comply
  • Must destroy by shredding, erasing or otherwise modifying personal information to make the information indecipherable
  • This staute doesn't define reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
Exceptions Does not apply to financial institutions or their affiliates under GLBA
