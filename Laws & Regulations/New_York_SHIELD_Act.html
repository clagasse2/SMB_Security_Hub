New York SHIELD Act

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization to develop, implement, and maintain reasonable safeguards to protect the security, confidentiality, and integrity of private information

Subject Entities Persons or entities that own or license computerized data which includes the private information of a New York resident
Penalty for Non-Compliance The Attorney General may bring an action for violation of the statute to enjoin violations and to obtain civil penalties
How to Comply
  • Implement reasonable administrative safeguards
  • Implement reasonable technical safeguards
  • Implement reasonable physical safeguards
Exceptions
  • A “small business” as defined by the statute is in compliance if its security program contains reasonable administrative, technical and physical safeguards that are appropriate for the size and complexity of the small business, the nature and scope of the small business’s activities, and the sensitivity of the personal information the small business collects from or about consumers
  • A person or entity that is subject to, and in compliance with, GLBA, HIPAA and HITECH, NYDFS Reg. 500, or other state or federal data security programs, are considered “compliant regulated entities.”
More Information N.Y. Gen. Bus. Law § 899-bb
