Indiana Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization to implement and maintain reasonable procedures, including taking any appropriate corrective action, to protect and safeguard from unlawful use or disclosure any personal information of Indiana residents

Subject Entities Applies to any entity that owns or licenses computerized data that includes personal information
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • Civil penalties under statue limited to $5,000 per violation of security or disposal standards
  • A violation of disposal standards for personal information of a “customer” may also result in prosecution under Ind. Code Ann. § 24-4-14-8, which is a Class C Infraction subject to a $500 judgment. If more than one hundred customers are affected or a repeat offense, the subject entity may be subject to a judgment of $10,000
How to Comply
  • Must dispose of records or documents containing unencrypted and unredacted personal information by shredding, incinerating, mutilating, erasing, or otherwise rendering the personal information illegible or unusable
  • This staute doesn't define "reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
Exceptions An organization that complies with the following is exempt
  • The PATRIOT Act
  • Executive Order 13224
  • The Drivers Privacy Protection Act
  • The Fair Credit Reporting Act
  • The Financial Modernization Act of 1999
  • The Health Insurance Portability and Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act
