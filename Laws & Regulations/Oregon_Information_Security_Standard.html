Oregon Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization to develop, implement, and maintain reasonable safeguards to protect the security, confidentiality, and integrity of personal information

Subject Entities Any person that owns, licenses, maintains, stores, manages, collects, possesses, acquires or otherwise possesses personal information in the course of the person’s business, vacation, occupation or volunteer activities. Subject entities do not include a person described above to the extent that person acts solely as a vendor
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • The Director of the Department of Consumer and Business Services may investigate as deemed necessary to determine whether a violation has occurred or to aid in enforcement
  • Violations shall be subject to a penalty of not more than $1,000 for every violation. Each violation is a separate offense, and each day of a continuing violation is a separate violation, but the maximum penalty for any occurrence is $500,000.May also result in civil penalties under ORS §183.745
How to Comply
  • Implement a Security Program that includes administrative, technical, and physical safeguards
  • Small business owners may comply if they have a security and disposal program that contains administrative, technical, and physical safeguards and disposal measures appropriate to their size, scope, and sensitivty of information collected
Exceptions
  • In compliance with GLBA
  • In compliance with HIPPA
  • In compliance with state or federal laws with greater protections for personal information than Oregon’s statute
