Texas Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires that an organization Must implement and maintain reasonable procedures, including taking any appropriate corrective action, to protect from unlawful use or disclosure of any sensitive personal information collected or maintained in the regular course of business

Subject Entities Businesses that collect or maintain personal identifying information
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • Violations relating to sensitive personal information may result in a civil penalty of at least $2,000 but not more than $50,000 for each violation
  • Improper disposal of a business record may be subject to a civil penalty up to $500 for each business record
  • The Attorney General may also recover reasonable attorney’s fees, court costs, and investigatory costs
  • The Attorney General may bring an action to recover civil penalties or to seek an injunction against persons who fail to take reasonable action to comply
How to Comply
  • Sensitive or personal identifying information may be destroyed by shredding, erasing, or otherwise modifying the information to make it unreadable or indecipherable. May comply with these statutes by contracting with another entity in the business of disposing of records
  • This staute doesn't define reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
Exceptions Financial institutions as defined by 15 U.S.C. 6809 and a covered entity as defined by sections 606.001 or 602.001 of the Texas Insurance Code are exempt
More Information Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §§ 72.002, 72.004
