South Carolina Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires that an organization develop, implement, and maintain a comprehensive written information security program based on a risk assessment and that contains administrative, technical, and physical safeguards for the protection of nonpublic information and the entity’s information system

Subject Entities Businesses and government entities
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • A willful violation by a person may be liable for three times the amount of actual damages or not more than one thousand dollars ($1,000) for each incident, whichever is greater, as well as reasonable attorney’s fees and costs
  • A negligent violation by a person is liable for actual damages and reasonable attorney’s fees and costs
How to Comply This staute doesn't define reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
Exceptions
  • A bank or financial institution subject to and in compliance with the GLBA
  • A health insurer subject to and in compliance with HIPAA
  • A consumer credit-reporting agency that is subject to and in compliance with the FCRA
