Nebraska Information Security Standard

This website provides general information and is not intended as legal advice


Summary

This regulation requires an organization to implement and maintain reasonable security procedures and practices to protect personal information from unauthorized access, acquisition, destruction, use, modification, or disclosure

Subject Entities Persons or entities that own, maintain, or license personal information of a Nebraska resident. Not applicable to state or local entities
Penalty for Non-Compliance
  • A violation of the information security requirements is considered a violation of the state Consumer Protection Act. The Attorney General may bring an action for violation of the statute
  • A violation may result in civil penalties and other remedies. Statute does not create a private right of action
How to Comply
  • Must require third-party service providers by contract to implement and maintain reasonable security procedures and practices appropriate to the nature of the personal information disclosed. Must be reasonably designed to help protect the information from unauthorized access, use, modification, disclosure, or destruction
  • This staute doesn't define reasonable security procedures and practices. It is recommended to comply with the Massachusetts Information Security Standard
Exceptions A subject entity may comply with requirements if it complies with a state or federal law that provides greater protections to personal information, or compliance with the regulations of GLBA or HIPAA
