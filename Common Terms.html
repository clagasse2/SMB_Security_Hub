Common Terms

An overview of terms frequently encountered.

Protected Health Information (PHI) or (ePHI)


Protected health information under U.S. law is any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that is created or collected by a Covered Entity, and can be linked to a specific individual

Personal Identifiable Information


Any representation of information that permits the identity of an individual to whom the information applies to be reasonably inferred by either direct or indirect means

Breach


Unauthorized access or acquisition to computerized data that compromises the security or confidentiality of personal information, excluding certain good faith acquisitions

Center for Internet Security Controls


The Center for Internet Security (CIS) Security Controls are a prioritized set of actions for cybersecurity that form a defense-in-depth set of specific and actionable best practices to mitigate the most common cyber attacks

Encryption


Encryption is the process of protecting information or data by using mathematical models to scramble it in such a way that only the parties who have the key to unscramble it can access it
